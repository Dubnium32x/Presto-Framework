# Presto Framework Design Document Future Plans
## Overview
This document outlines the future plans for the Presto framework, focusing on enhancements, new features, and long-term goals. It serves as a roadmap for developers to understand the direction of the framework and prioritize development efforts.

## Planned Enhancements
### Performance Optimization
- **Profiling Tools**: Implement profiling tools to identify performance bottlenecks in the framework. This will help developers optimize critical sections of code and improve overall performance.
- **Memory Management**: Enhance memory management systems to reduce fragmentation and improve allocation speed. This may include implementing custom allocators or garbage collection strategies tailored to the needs of the framework.
- **Rendering Pipeline**: No need to optimize the rendering pipeline for better performance, especially for high-resolution textures and complex scenes. Raylib already provides a solid foundation, but future updates may include support for advanced rendering techniques like deferred shading or dynamic lighting.

### Cross-Platform Support
- **Different Platforms**: We will want to have support for PC operating systems, such as Windows, macOS, and Linux. This will involve testing and optimizing the framework for each platform's unique characteristics.

## Making an Actual Sonic Fan Game with this Framework
### A Look Ahead
- **Game Templates**: Develop templates for common Sonic game types, such as 2D side-scrollers or 3D platformers. These templates will provide a starting point for developers and showcase the framework's capabilities.
- **Sample Games**: Create sample games that demonstrate the framework's features and best practices. This will help new developers understand how to use the framework effectively and inspire them to create their own games.

### Community Engagement
- **Feedback Loop**: Establish a feedback loop with the community to gather input on features, enhancements, and bug fixes. This can include forums, surveys, or direct communication channels.
- **Contributions**: Encourage community contributions by providing clear guidelines for submitting code, reporting issues, and suggesting features. This will help foster a collaborative environment and ensure that the framework evolves to meet the needs of its users.
- **Documentation**: Continuously update and improve documentation to reflect new features, changes, and best practices. This will help developers quickly find the information they need and reduce the learning curve for new users.

## Conclusion
The future plans for the Presto framework focus on enhancing performance, expanding cross-platform support, and fostering community engagement. By implementing these enhancements and actively involving the community, the framework can continue to evolve and provide a robust foundation for creating Sonic fan games and other projects. Continuous iteration and feedback will ensure that the Presto framework remains relevant and effective in meeting the needs of its users.
